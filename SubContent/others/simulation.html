Simulation of the PDL-osteocytes crosstalk

Presumptions

Suppose that

The diffusion equation for the solids concentration in a solvent is given by

$$ \frac{ \partial \varphi}{ \partial t } = D \frac{ \partial^{2} \varphi }{ \partial x^{2} } + S $$

where \(\varphi\) is the concentration of some factors (e.g. sclerostin) with the unit of nanomolar (nM); \(t\) is the time with unit of second (s); \(D\) is the diffusion coefficient with the unit of \(\mu m^{2}/s\); and \(x\) is the position that indicates the distance to the boundary of PDL.

This equation can solved using finite difference method giving

$$ \frac{ \partial \varphi}{ \partial t } = D \frac{ \partial^{2} \varphi }{ \partial x^{2} } + S $$
