PDL induced change of circadian rhythm in osteocyte could change the spatial distribution of osteocyte produced protein

Presumptions

Suppose a 85\(\mu\) length one dimensional even space with 17 evenly distributated osteocytes on a interval of 5\(\mu\). Each osteocyte produces sclerostin following the sine function with a period of 24 hours. Meanwhile, each osteocyte could consume a certain amount of sclerostin in a stable level.

This one dimensional space will strat with PDL cells. Those cells could produce some factors that only change the phase of the sine function that control the production of sclerostin in osteocytes. This influence is concentration-dependent.

There are no flow in this space, and all solids (PDL produced factors and sclerostin) are simply diffusive in this space without interactions to each other.

Equations

The diffusion equation for the solids concentration in a solvent is given by

$$ \frac{ \partial \varphi}{ \partial t } = D \frac{ \partial^{2} \varphi }{ \partial x^{2} } + S $$

where \(\varphi\) is the concentration of some factors (e.g. sclerostin) with the unit of nanomolar (nM); \(t\) is the time with unit of second (s); \(D\) is the diffusion coefficient with the unit of \(\mu m^{2}/s\); and \(x\) is the position that indicates the distance to the boundary of PDL.

This equation can solved using finite difference method giving

$$ \frac{C^{(n+1)}_{i}-C^{(n)}_{i}}{\varDelta t} = D \cdot \frac{C^{(n)}_{i+1}-2\cdot C^{(n)}_{i}+C^{(n)}_{i-1}}{\varDelta x^2}+S_{i} $$

where \(C\) is the solids concentration scalar field; \(n\) is time; \(i\) is x-axis indices; and \(S\) is the resource. We can rearrage this equation to get

$$ C^{(n+1)}_{i}=C^{(n)}_{i}+\varDelta t\cdot D \cdot \frac{C^{(n)}_{i+1}-2 \cdot C^{(n)}_{i}+C^{(n)}_{i-1}}{\varDelta x^2}+S_{i} $$

Since we assume that each osteocyte produces sclerostin following the sine function with a period of 24 hours and consumes a certain amount of sclerostin in a stable level. It could be gaven by

$$ S= 0.07 picomolar\cdot[\sin(\frac{2\cdot \pi\cdot 3600\cdot t}{24}+\alpha\cdot\pi)+0.5] $$

where \(\alpha\) is the ratio of PDL produced factors concentration on current position to the boundary condition of PDL produced factors with higher value. Its range is \(0\leq\alpha\leq 1\).

Initial Condition for \(pC\)

where the prefix \(p\) denotes the \(pC\) as the concentration of PDL produced factors

$$ pC_{(x, 0)}=0, \quad 0\leq x\leq 85 $$

Boundary Condition for \(pC\)

Before force load

$$ pC_{(x, 0)}=0, \quad 0\leq x\leq 85 $$

After force load

$$ pC_{(0, t)}=10 \ nM \\ pC_{(85, t)}=0, \quad t\geq 0 $$

Initial Condition for \(oC\)

where the prefix \(o\) denotes the \(oC\) as the concentration of osteocyte produced sclerostin

$$ oC_{(x, 0)}=0.17 \ nM, \quad 0\leq x\leq 85 $$

Boundary Condition for \(oC\)

$$ oC_{(0, t)}=oC_{(85, t)}=0.17 \ nM, \quad t\geq 0 $$
